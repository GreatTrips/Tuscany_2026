Optional Split: Basilica di Santa Croce ℹ Optional

📍 Piazza Santa Croce — 15-min walk from the Airbnb. For those who want more than breakfast before the Uffizi, Santa Croce is the most walkable detour.

  • What it is: The largest Franciscan church in the world. Known as the "Temple of Italian Glories" — the burial place of Michelangelo, Galileo, Machiavelli, and Rossini
  • Frescoes: Giotto's stunning Bardi and Peruzzi chapel frescoes (14th century) — some of the finest surviving medieval paintings in Italy
  • Michelangelo's tomb: Just inside the main door on the right. Designed by Vasari. Ironic note: Michelangelo always wanted to be buried in Rome
  • Galileo's tomb: Across the aisle. He was denied a Christian burial for decades due to his heresy conviction — this monument was only erected in 1737, nearly a century after his death
  • Cost: ~€8. No Passepartout (that's the Duomo complex only)
Split suggestion: Those who want Santa Croce can break away at 9am, spend 45 mins, and walk directly to the Uffizi (12 min) to meet the group at 10:15 AM. A perfectly manageable split before the main group event.
10:00 AM

Entire Group (or reconvene) — Departs for Uffizi

Uffizi Gallery ℹ ✓ PASSEPARTOUT ACTIVATED

World's greatest collection of Renaissance art. Botticelli's Birth of Venus, Titian, Caravaggio, and more. Allow the full 2.5 hours.

Passepartout: This 5-day pass activates TODAY. Keep the physical ticket safe for the rest of the week. Audio guide available for €6 until 5pm.

The Street Food Duo

  • All'Antico Vinaio: World-renowned Florentine schiacciata sandwiches. Try "La Favolosa" — balanced truffle and pecorino flavors. 3-min walk from Uffizi
    • ⚠ Street eating ban: Via de' Neri (where All'Antico Vinaio is located) is a restricted zone — no stopping to eat on the street between noon–3 PM and 6–10 PM. Fine: €150–€500. Buy your sandwich, then walk 5–8 minutes to the Lungarno riverbank, Piazza della Signoria, or any piazza to eat. Do not stand and eat outside the shop.
  • I' Girone De' Ghiotti: Try Lampredotto (slow-cooked tripe) — true Florentine street food
Pollini Lampredotto (Sant'Ambrogio): Closed Sundays and Mondays. If you want this, go Saturday after the David or Tuesday morning before car pickup.

Car Pickup — Borgo Ognissanti

Volterra Must-Dos ℹ

  • Guarnacci Etruscan Museum (~€9): The "Shadow of the Evening" bronze — inspired Giacometti. Start at top floor, work down. 600+ ancient funerary urns. Recently renovated
  • Pinacoteca: Rosso Fiorentino's Deposizione (1521) — a startling Mannerist masterpiece. Almost nobody knows it exists
  • Porta all'Arco: 3rd century BC Etruscan arch — still standing after 2,300 years. Free
  • Roman Theatre lookout: Free panoramic view from Viale Francesco Ferrucci (closed Wed for ticketed entry)
4:30 PM2h 15m

  • Rossi Alabaster: Working lathe next door — free to watch. Items €3–€3,500
  • Le Balze cliffs: 15-min walk from centre — medieval church sliding over erosion cliffs. Free. Eerie and spectacular
Le Balze: best shot from the viewpoint looking northwest at dusk, with the cliffs dropping away and the countryside beyond.
